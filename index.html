Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

The People's President and the Missile Man of India

About Abdul Kalam

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam was an aerospace scientist who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. Known as the "Missile Man" for his work on the development of ballistic missile technology, he played a pivotal role in India’s nuclear tests in 1998. His humility, dedication to education, and contributions to science made him a beloved figure in India.

Key Achievements

  • Developed India’s first satellite launch vehicle (SLV-III).
  • Led India’s successful nuclear tests in 1998.
  • Authored several inspirational books including "Wings of Fire."
  • Recipient of Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian honor.
  • Inspired millions of students with his educational outreach.
